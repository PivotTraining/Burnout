## ⚠️ CRITICAL FINDING — Trademark / Naming Collision

While capturing the baseline I discovered that another company is already
operating under the name **BurnoutIQ™** with the trademark symbol claimed.

**The competitor:**

- **Company:** Five to Flow® (founded 2020 by Kate Visconti)
- **Primary domain:** [fivetoflow.com](https://fivetoflow.com/)
- **Product domain:** [discover-flow.com](https://discover-flow.com/) — hosts their "free BurnoutIQ™ assessment"
- **Positioning:** 5–10 minute confidential assessment built on Maslach's 6 workplace drivers (workload, control, reward, community, fairness, values) — *the same six dimensions we frame Pivot's BurnoutIQ around*
- **PR cycle:** PRNewswire launch dated 2024–2025 ("BurnoutIQ™ to Address Its $8.9 Trillion Toll") + follow-on coverage in HRTechCube, AI Journal, Fox5 San Diego
- **Trademark status:** They're using the ™ symbol publicly; USPTO registration status not confirmed (Chris — recommend an IP attorney pull this within the week)

**Why this matters:**

1. **SEO:** They have a 12+ month head start in news/PR coverage. "BurnoutIQ" as a brand search will be increasingly contested.
2. **Sales:** A CHRO Googling "BurnoutIQ" lands on their PRNewswire piece first, not us.
3. **Legal:** If they file a USPTO registration first and we keep marketing under the same name, we lose. ™ alone is just a claim; ® is enforceable.
4. **AI engines:** ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini all train on PRNewswire + the open web. They will conflate "BurnoutIQ" with Five to Flow unless we differentiate aggressively.

**Recommended actions this week (in order):**

1. **Pull USPTO record** for "BurnoutIQ" via [tmsearch.uspto.gov](https://tmsearch.uspto.gov/) — confirm if Five to Flow has filed or just claimed.
2. **IP attorney consult** — 30 minutes, ~$300. Two questions: (a) can we file BurnoutIQ as our mark if they haven't registered? (b) if they have, what naming change is safest?
3. **Defensive naming options to think about** (in order of least disruptive):
   - "BurnoutIQ by Pivot" — common-law differentiation, still searchable
   - "Pivot BurnoutIQ" — same idea, slightly stronger
   - Rename the *product* but keep the diagnostic methodology — e.g., "PressureIQ Burnout" or "The Pivot Burnout Index"
4. **Don't ship more PR with bare "BurnoutIQ" until #1–2 are answered.** Every new article makes the unwind harder.

This is the single highest-leverage thing to resolve before pushing the 30-day SEO sprint live. SEO investment on a name we may need to change is wasted spend.
